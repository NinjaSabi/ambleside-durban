There is a question we get asked often by prospective families: "How big are your classes?"

Our answer is always the same: small. Deliberately, convictionally small.

This is not a circumstance we are working around. It is a choice we have made — and it shapes everything about how Ambleside works.

When a teacher has a small class, they do not just know their students' names. They know what each child is ready for. They know which child needs encouragement today and which one needs a gentle challenge. They know when something is wrong before a child says a word. That knowledge is not a nice extra — it is the engine of good teaching.

Charlotte Mason believed that education happens through relationship. A teacher who genuinely knows a child can offer them a genuinely human education. A teacher managing a class of thirty cannot — no matter how skilled or dedicated they are.

At Ambleside School of Durban, we guard our class sizes carefully. We would rather remain small and do this well than grow and lose the thing that makes us what we are.

If you are looking for a school where your child will truly be known — we would love to meet your family.
