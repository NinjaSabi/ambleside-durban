Charlotte Mason (1842–1923) was a British educator whose ideas were revolutionary in her time and remain urgently relevant today. At their heart is a simple but radical conviction: children deserve better than rote learning, narrow curricula, and education reduced to test results.

Mason believed that education is "an atmosphere, a discipline, a life." By atmosphere she meant the environment in which children learn — warm, ordered, and rich with ideas. By discipline she meant the formation of good habits: attention, honesty, perseverance. By life she meant that education is not preparation for living — it is living itself.

At Ambleside School of Durban, this shapes everything. We use living books — written by people who genuinely love their subjects. We practise narration — children retelling what they have encountered, making it their own. We spend time outdoors, keep nature notebooks, study a composer each term, and offer a broad curriculum that takes history, literature, science, art, music, and languages seriously.

We do not teach to CAPS. Our students are well-prepared for mainstream high school — but that is not the primary aim. The aim is to form people who love learning, think clearly, and live well.
