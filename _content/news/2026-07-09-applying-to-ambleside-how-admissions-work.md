Choosing a school isn't really choosing a building or a fee structure. It's choosing the environment your child will spend their days inside, and the people who will help shape how they see the world. We don't take that lightly, and we don't expect you to either.

Ambleside is a small school by conviction, not by accident. That means we don't run a numbers-driven admissions machine. We would far rather get to know your family, and let you get to know us, before anyone signs anything.

Here is how it actually works.

## Start with a conversation, not a form

The best first step isn't paperwork. It's coming to see us. You're welcome to [book a visit](/contact/), or simply pop in to one of our Thursday open mornings. Come and see the school while it's breathing: children at work, lessons in progress, the ordinary rhythm of a normal day.

## Come and see the classroom in motion

We teach in the Charlotte Mason tradition: short, focused lessons, living books rather than worksheets, narration instead of rote recall, and real time outdoors. It's a particular way of educating, and it's much easier to understand by watching it than by reading about it. When you visit, we'll show you a lesson and talk you through what you're seeing.

## Apply when you're ready

When it feels right, you can begin a formal application through our [How to Apply](/admissions/how-to-apply/) page. You'll find everything we ask for there, along with our current [fees](/admissions/fees/). There is no rush and no pressure. Apply when your family is ready, not before.

## A conversation about fit

Because we're small, every child here is genuinely known. So part of admissions is an honest conversation about whether Ambleside is the right home for your child. We'll talk about your family's values and what you're hoping for. If we're a good fit, you'll feel it. If we're not, we'll say so kindly. The right school matters more than a full register.

## An offer, and a welcome

Once a place is confirmed, the paperwork is quick and the welcome is warm. And if fees are a genuine obstacle for a family who shares our values, we'd encourage you to ask about our [Bursary Fund](/donate/). We believe an Ambleside education should reach every family we can.

If any of this resonates, we'd love to meet you. [Book a visit](/contact/) or [start your application](/admissions/how-to-apply/) whenever you're ready.
