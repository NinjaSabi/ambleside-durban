Homeschool Community

Thursday Enrichment
Programme

A weekly morning for homeschooled children — rich learning, real community, and the joy of doing it together.

For Homeschool Families

The best parts of school,
without leaving home.

You’ve built something remarkable. Homeschooling your children takes courage, commitment, and deep love — and if you’re reading this, you probably already know that the Ambleside philosophy resonates with what you’re trying to do at home.

The Thursday Enrichment Programme is our way of opening our doors to the homeschool community. Not to convince you to change what’s working — but to offer something that’s genuinely hard to replicate at home: peer community, specialist-led subjects, and the particular joy that comes from learning alongside others.

Come for a Thursday. Stay for the term. See what happens.

What Happens on Thursdays

Three hours of living learning.

Every Thursday session follows the same warm rhythm — three activities that are genuinely difficult to do well alone at home, but come alive in community.

Nature Study

Children head outdoors with notebooks in hand — observing, sketching, and recording what they find. Charlotte Mason believed careful attention to the natural world was the foundation of all good scientific thinking, and we take that seriously. No two Thursdays look the same.

Composer Study & Music Appreciation

We spend unhurried time with a single composer — listening carefully, talking about what we hear, and learning to receive music as something meaningful. This term we are getting to know a composer whose work rewards slow, attentive listening. No prior musical experience needed.

Play & Peer Interaction

One of the things homeschooling families tell us they miss most is simply — other children. Unstructured play with peers of different ages is genuinely valuable, and we protect time for it every Thursday. Children make friends here. Parents do too.

A Word to Homeschool Families

You’ve done something remarkable.

Whether you’ve been homeschooling for one year or seven, whether you’re thriving or wondering what comes next — this programme is for you. It’s a space to connect, to enrich, and to explore whether Ambleside might be a community worth knowing better. There is no pressure. Just a warm door, open on Thursdays.
