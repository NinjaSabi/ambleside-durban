Our Philosophy

The Charlotte Mason
Philosophy

An education that treats children as whole persons — and learning as a feast, not a formula.

Who Was Charlotte Mason?

An Educator Who Changed
Everything.

Charlotte Mason (1842–1923) was a British educator who developed a revolutionary approach to education — one built on the conviction that children are born persons, capable of engaging with the best ideas humanity has produced. Her approach rejected the dry, rote-learning methods of her time in favour of living books, direct encounters with nature, narration, and a broad, generous curriculum.

More than a century later, her ideas are more relevant than ever. In a world of screens, standardised tests, and an increasingly narrow view of what education is for, Charlotte Mason's vision of education as the "science of relations" offers a compelling alternative.

"The question is not — how much does the youth know when he has finished his education — but how much does he care?"
Charlotte M. Mason
How It Looks at Ambleside

Living Ideas in Practice

Living Books

We use books written by authors who love their subjects — not dry textbooks. When a child reads a living book, they encounter a real mind engaging with real ideas, and that changes them.

Narration

Instead of tests and worksheets, children narrate — they tell back, in their own words, what they have read or heard. This simple practice builds comprehension, memory, and the ability to think clearly.

Nature Study

Regular time outdoors, nature notebooks, and careful observation of the natural world. Children who learn to see the world around them develop a capacity for wonder that lasts a lifetime.

Composer & Picture Study

Each term, children spend time with one composer and one visual artist — listening carefully, looking closely, and developing an aesthetic sensibility that enriches their whole life.

Broad Curriculum

History, literature, science, languages, mathematics, art, music, handwork — all of it. We refuse to narrow the curriculum to what can be easily tested. Children deserve the full feast.

Atmosphere & Habit

The environment matters. The habits children form matter. A calm, ordered, warm atmosphere is not a luxury — it is the soil in which real learning grows.

Ambleside Schools International

Part of a Global Family.

Ambleside Durban North is a member of Ambleside Schools International — a network of Charlotte Mason schools across the world, sharing curriculum, teacher training, and a common commitment to this vision of education.
