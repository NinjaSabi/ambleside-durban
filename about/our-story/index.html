Our Story

Our Story

A school born from conviction — that children deserve better than worksheets and rote learning.

How It Began

A Small School with a
Big Conviction.

Ambleside Durban North was founded on a simple but radical idea: that children are not vessels to be filled with information, but whole persons deserving of a rich, broad, and deeply human education.

We are part of the Ambleside Schools International (ASI) network — a global family of schools united by the educational philosophy of Charlotte Mason. ASI schools share a common curriculum framework, teacher training, and a commitment to the same vision: that education is not primarily about outcomes or results, but about the formation of the whole person.

"Education is an atmosphere, a discipline, a life."
Charlotte M. Mason

Durban North, Our Home

We are rooted in the Durban North community — a small school where every family is known by name, and where the relationships between teachers, children, and parents are genuine and lasting. We believe that education works best in community, and that the school-family partnership is not optional — it is essential.

Our campus is a place where children are unhurried. Where there is time for a nature walk, for a long read-aloud, for a conversation that goes somewhere unexpected. We guard that unhurriedness carefully, because we believe it is in those unrushed moments that the deepest learning happens.

Two Schools, One Vision

We offer two programmes under one roof: Ambleside Preschool — Acorns for children from Grade 000 to Grade R, and Ambleside Primary School for Grades 1 to 7. Both are shaped by the same Charlotte Mason philosophy, ensuring that a child's journey from their earliest years right through to Grade 7 is coherent, warm, and deeply intentional.
