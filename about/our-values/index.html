Our Values

Our Values

Not aspirations on a wall — lived convictions that shape every decision we make.

01

Children Are Persons

Each child who walks through our doors is a whole person — not a statistic, not a grade, and not a project. They arrive with their own inner life, their own curiosity, their own dignity. Our job is to honour that, and to educate the whole child: mind, body, and spirit.

02

Education Is a Feast

We refuse to offer children a narrow, impoverished curriculum. Learning at Ambleside is broad and generous — literature, history, science, nature study, music, art, handwork, languages, mathematics. We believe children can handle far more richness than most schools give them credit for.

03

Character Over Credentials

We care deeply about academic excellence — but we care more about who our students are becoming. Diligence, honesty, kindness, courage, and a genuine love of learning are the outcomes we measure ourselves against. Small classes make real character formation possible.

04

Families Are Partners

Education is a whole-family journey. We keep our partnership with parents warm, honest, and mutual — sharing regularly, listening carefully, and treating parents as essential participants in their child's formation, not merely as observers.

05

A Living Faith

Our Christian ethos is not a bolt-on. It is the framework through which we understand people, the world, and our purpose. We take the life and teaching of Jesus seriously — and we believe that a Christ-centred education is a richer, truer, and more human education.

06

Unhurried Learning

We resist the pressure to rush children through their education. We believe in giving children time — time to be curious, time to wonder, time to read slowly, time to play. The best formation cannot be hurried, and we will not pretend otherwise.
