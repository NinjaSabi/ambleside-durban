Our Values

Our Values

Not aspirations on a wall — lived convictions that shape every decision we make.

01

Children Are Persons

Each child who walks through our doors is a whole person — not a statistic, not a grade, and not a project. They arrive with their own inner life, their own curiosity, their own dignity. Our job is to honour that, and to educate the whole child: mind, body, and spirit.

02

Education Is a Feast

We refuse to offer children a narrow, outcomes-driven curriculum. Learning at Ambleside is broad and generous — literature, history, science, nature study, music, art, handwork, languages, mathematics. We believe children can handle far more richness than we often give them credit for.

03

Character Over Credentials

We pursue excellence in both character and academics — because we believe the two are inseparable. Diligence, honesty, kindness, and courage are not soft alternatives to academic rigour; they are the very qualities that make genuine learning possible. Small classes allow us to hold both well, knowing every child by name and nurturing who they are becoming alongside what they know.

04

Families Are Partners

We believe that parents are the primary educators of their children — and we take that seriously. Our role is to come alongside you, not replace you. We actively partner with families in both the education and formation of each child, sharing openly, listening carefully, and keeping the relationship warm, honest, and genuinely mutual.

05

A Living Faith

Our Christian ethos is not a bolt-on. It is the framework through which we understand people, the world, and our purpose. We take the life and teaching of Jesus seriously — and we believe that a Christ-centred education is a richer, truer, and more human education.

06

Unhurried Learning

We resist the pressure to rush children through their education. We believe in giving children time — time to be curious, time to wonder, time to read slowly, time to play. The best formation cannot be hurried, and we take that seriously in every decision we make.
