Junior School

Junior School
Curriculum

A broad, rich curriculum built on Charlotte Mason principles — and the best that each subject has to offer.

Charlotte Mason believed that children deserve a wide and generous curriculum — not a narrow diet of literacy and numeracy, but a genuine feast of ideas across every area of human knowledge. That belief shapes everything we teach at Ambleside Junior School.

M

Mathematics — Singapore Math

We use the Singapore Mathematics approach — widely regarded as the most effective primary maths curriculum in the world. Singapore Math develops deep conceptual understanding before moving to procedures, building children who can genuinely think mathematically rather than merely follow steps. Combined with mental maths practice and problem-solving, our students develop real mathematical confidence.

L

Language & Literacy

English is taught through rich literature, living books, narration, dictation, copywork, and composition. We believe that children who read widely and well, and who are taught to express themselves clearly, have a foundation for everything else. Grammar and spelling are taught in context — not as isolated drill.

H

History & Geography

History is taught chronologically and through living books — biographies, primary sources, and narrative histories rather than textbook summaries. Geography moves from the local to the global, building a genuine sense of place. Mapwork, timelines, and narration help children internalise what they learn.

S

Science & Nature Study

Science at Ambleside begins outside. Nature notebooks, careful observation, and time in the natural world build the habits of attention that underpin all scientific thinking. Formal science builds on this foundation — teaching children to ask good questions, observe carefully, and think clearly about the physical world.

A

Arts — Music, Picture Study & Handwork

Each term, students spend time with one composer and one visual artist — listening carefully, looking closely, developing aesthetic sensibility. Handwork (sewing, woodwork, crafts) develops fine motor skills and the capacity to make things well. These are not extras. They are part of the feast.

Z

Languages — Afrikaans & isiZulu

South Africa is a multilingual country, and we believe that children should be equipped to engage with their context. Afrikaans is taught from Grade 1; isiZulu is introduced in the junior grades. Language learning at Ambleside is oral and relational — rooted in conversation, song, and story before formal grammar.

B

Bible & Character

Bible is taught as a living book — through story, narration, and discussion. We are not trying to produce children who know the right answers; we are trying to introduce them to the person of Jesus Christ and the story of redemption. Character formation — honesty, diligence, courage, kindness — is woven into the fabric of every day.
